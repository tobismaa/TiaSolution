Executive Summary

Overview

Tia is a cloud-based finance operating system designed to help businesses manage core financial workflows from a single controlled workspace. The platform is built to support multiple organizations, multiple branches, and multiple user roles, making it suitable for businesses that need operational discipline as they scale.

The solution combines finance administration, operational workflows, reporting, access management, and audit visibility in one product. Rather than relying on scattered spreadsheets, disconnected tools, and manual follow-up, clients can operate through a structured platform with role-based controls and traceable activity.

Many growing businesses struggle to maintain control over finance processes as teams, branches, and transaction volumes increase. Approvals become inconsistent, reporting is delayed, access is loosely managed, and audit preparation becomes expensive and stressful. Tia addresses this gap by giving finance teams, management, and oversight users a shared platform with defined workflows and controlled visibility.

User Model

Role-Based Operating Structure

Tia is designed around a role-sensitive operating model. This is a major strength of the product and should be highlighted clearly in the proposal because it demonstrates governance, scalability, and practical usability across different business responsibilities.

Super Admin

Platform-level workspace for managing organizations, demo requests, subscriptions, and platform settings.

Business Admin

Business-level control over branches, payroll, users, and organization-wide setup.

Manager / Head of Operations

Operational oversight, reporting access, and GL-related visibility for business leaders.

Staff / Operations

Frontline operational use for day-to-day workflows under controlled access boundaries.

Auditor

Read-only visibility into reports, ledgers, and audit-related information to support oversight and review.

Account

Finance-focused role with access to payroll, trial balance, ledgers, and broader accounting workflows.

Technology

Technical Architecture and Security Approach

Tia is structured as a modular web application with separate presentation pages, role-aware workspaces, shared utilities, modular business features, and a Supabase-backed data layer. This architecture is appropriate for phased delivery because it allows new modules and workflows to be expanded without rebuilding the core platform.

The backend model is designed for multi-tenant usage, with organization-level data isolation and table structures that align with finance and operations workflows.

Architecture Highlights

  • Frontend built with modular HTML, CSS, and JavaScript
  • Supabase authentication and database integration
  • Role-based routing and workspace rendering
  • Row-level data isolation for business tenants
  • Support for demo, trial, and live operating modes

Delivery Model

Recommended Implementation Approach

Because the product foundation already exists, the recommended approach is not to restart from scratch but to move through a structured completion and deployment roadmap. This reduces waste, accelerates time to value, and allows the client to benefit from the existing architecture while refining the remaining business workflows.

Phase Focus Indicative Activities Outcome
Phase 1 Foundation Stabilization Validate live data connections, confirm role access, finalize organization onboarding, and harden navigation and permissions. Stable core platform ready for controlled pilot usage.
Phase 2 Workflow Completion Complete create and edit forms for invoices, expenses, and operational data capture. Refine approval-related workflows. Usable end-to-end operational workflow for key finance activities.
Phase 3 Finance Depth Enhance chart of accounts support, reporting, trial balance workflows, and accounting controls. Stronger accounting credibility and deeper reporting value.
Phase 4 Commercial and Scale Readiness Billing automation, subscription enforcement, support workflows, platform reporting, and client rollout processes. Deployment-ready SaaS operating model with lifecycle management.

Indicative Timeline

A realistic client engagement would treat implementation as a phased rollout rather than a single delivery event. Exact timing should be finalized after requirements confirmation, but the current product maturity supports a staged delivery model with early pilot visibility followed by deeper finance capability in later phases.
